Advance reader copies are still one of the cleanest ways to prepare a book launch. But many authors get nervous about one question: can ARC readers leave Amazon reviews?

The short answer is yes, if the review is honest, optional, and not tied to payment or pressure.

## What Makes An ARC Review Safer

An ARC reader should receive the book early so they can read it and share genuine feedback. The review must be their own opinion. You should not tell them what rating to leave, what phrases to use, or when they must post.

A clean request sounds simple: if you enjoyed the book, an honest review on Amazon would help future readers. That is very different from asking for a five-star review.

## What Authors Should Avoid

Do not offer money, refunds, gift cards, bonuses, or future favors for a review. Do not create a review swap group where authors trade praise. Do not ask readers to copy the same review across platforms.

The safest ARC program is small, transparent, and reader-led. You can remind readers once or twice, but the review itself must remain voluntary.

## A Better Launch Habit

Keep a simple ARC list, send the book early, and give readers a neutral reminder near launch week. A few thoughtful reviews from real early readers are worth more than a larger number that looks coordinated.

Amazon review trust is fragile. Treat ARC readers like readers, not like a ratings machine.
